Step 2 ‚Äì Self-hosting or a free alternative?


Whoa, slow down there! This is the biggest decision you‚Äôll have to make before we go any further. You need to decide whether to pay for your blog or grab a free one.


WordPress, Tumblr, and Blogger all offer free blogs for anyone. Awesome, right? It‚Äôs perfect for those of us who aren‚Äôt super serious about blogging. But it does have downsides:


1) You won‚Äôt be able to get your OWN domain name


On a free blog, your blog‚Äôs web address (your URL) will be butt-ugly. Like, really ugly. In short, create a free blog with any other the above free blog services and it‚Äôll look like this:

  * yourblog.wordpress.com

  * yourblog.tumblr.com

I know, ugly right?

It might sound silly at first, but you don‚Äôt actually own your blog. It‚Äôs hosted on someone else‚Äôs web property and they can delete it if they want to. On the other hand, with a self-hosted blog on your own domain name ‚Äì you are the REAL owner of your blog. You‚Äôll be able to name your blog whatever you want, for example, ‚ÄúYourName.com‚Äù or ‚ÄúYourAwesomeBlog.com. You can end it with .com, .co.uk, .net, .org, or virtually any other web suffix. So how much is hosting and a domain name? Not as much as you‚Äôre thinking, fortunately. It usually works out to about $5 to $10 per month, depending on your hosting provider which is less than a couple of coffees.
